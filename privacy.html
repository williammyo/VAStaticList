Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 12, 2025

This Privacy Policy outlines the data handling practices of the VA Disability Static List Chrome Extension ("the Extension"). This extension is developed and maintained by an individual developer and is not affiliated with or endorsed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA).

1. Overview

The Extension is a browser tool designed to enhance the readability of publicly accessible information on VA.gov by displaying structured data related to service-connected disabilities, such as static indicators, ratings, and diagnostic codes. The Extension operates entirely within the user's browser and does not collect, transmit, or store any personal or sensitive information.

2. Data Collection and Use

No personal data is collected. The Extension does not collect, transmit, sync, store, or share any data with external servers or third parties. All processing occurs locally on the user’s device.

  • No tracking cookies are used
  • No IP addresses, names, emails, or VA credentials are accessed or recorded
  • No user interactions are logged

3. Permissions

The Extension requires access to VA.gov claim pages in order to display data available to the logged-in user. It uses Chrome’s activeTab and scripting APIs solely for the purpose of parsing and presenting data already visible on the page to the user. These permissions are declared transparently in the manifest.json file and are required only while the user is actively browsing VA.gov.

4. Compliance with Chrome Web Store Policy

This extension complies with Google’s Chrome Web Store User Data Policy. Specifically:

  • Data is not sold, shared, or transferred to third parties
  • No data is used for advertising or marketing purposes
  • The extension does not access beyond the scope necessary for core functionality

5. Compliance with Global Privacy Regulations

Although no data is collected or stored, the Extension aligns with global privacy standards, including:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR – Regulation (EU) 2016/679) – No personal data is processed under Article 4, and no consent is required under Article 6.
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA – Cal. Civ. Code §§ 1798.100–1798.199) – No personal information is collected or sold, and users retain full privacy and control over their data.

6. Security

Because this extension does not transmit or store any data, it minimizes all risk associated with user information leakage. It runs entirely client-side and does not maintain any backend services or databases.

7. Disclaimer

The Extension is not affiliated with or endorsed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs or any government agency. It is intended for informational and convenience purposes only. Users are encouraged to verify any disability claim details with official VA channels.

8. Contact

For questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please reach out through the contact form on the Chrome Web Store listing or send a message via GitHub at https://github.com/williammyo/VAStaticList.
